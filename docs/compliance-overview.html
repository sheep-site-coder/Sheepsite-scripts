Florida Condo Website Compliance

How SheepSite.com Keeps Your Association Compliant

The Law: What You Need to Know

Starting January 1, 2026, Florida Statute 718.111(12) requires all condominium associations with 25 or more units to maintain a website or web portal.

This isn't optional—it's the law.

What the Law Requires

The statute specifies that certain documents must be posted on your association's website:

Documents That Must Be Publicly Accessible

These documents must be available to anyone, not just unit owners:

DocumentDescription
Articles of IncorporationYour association's founding document
BylawsRules governing the association
Rules and RegulationsCommunity rules
Declaration of CondominiumThe master deed/declaration
Frequently Asked Questions & AnswersCommon questions about the community

Documents for Members Only (Password-Protected)

These documents must be available to unit owners only:

DocumentDescription

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Is a simple website really enough to comply with the law?

Yes. The statute requires a "website or application" that provides access to the specified documents. Our service meets these requirements by providing both public access and authenticated (password-protected) access to the appropriate documents.

Q: Do we need a fancy, expensive website?

No. The law specifies what documents must be available, not how elaborate the website must be. A clean, functional website that provides easy access to required documents is fully compliant.

Q: What if we don't have all the required documents yet?

Post what you have and add documents as they become available. The important thing is to have a functioning website and a process for keeping it updated.

Q: Who is responsible if we miss posting a document?

The association's board is ultimately responsible for compliance. We provide the platform and make it easy to post documents, but we cannot monitor what you do or don't upload.

Q: Can unit owners download the documents?

Yes. All documents on your website can be viewed and downloaded by visitors (public documents) or authenticated members (private documents).

Q: Why does search matter if the law just requires documents to be posted?

Technically, the law only requires that documents be accessible — it doesn't require them to be easy to find. But in practice, when owners can't quickly locate what they're looking for, they call the board. Our powerful search lets any owner find any document with a single query, across the entire document store. Fewer calls, fewer emails, less work for the board.

Q: Is our data secure?

Your documents are stored on secure servers with industry-standard protection. The member-only section requires individual owner accounts to access. All websites include SSL encryption (https://).
