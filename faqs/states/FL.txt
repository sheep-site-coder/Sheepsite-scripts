# Florida Condominium Law — Owner FAQ

## What law governs Florida condominiums?
Florida Statute Chapter 718 (the "Condominium Act") governs all condominiums in Florida.
The association must operate in compliance with both Chapter 718 and its own governing documents.

## What documents must be posted on the association website?
Under Florida Statute 718.111(12), associations with 25 or more units must maintain a website
with the following documents:
- Declaration of Condominium and all amendments
- Bylaws and all amendments
- Rules and Regulations
- Current year budget and most recent annual financial report
- Frequently asked questions (FAQ) sheet
- All board meeting agendas and minutes (for the past 7 years)
- All financial reports and budgets (for the past 7 years)
- Names and contact info for all board members and association representatives

## Can I inspect association records?
Yes. Florida Statute 718.111(12) gives owners the right to inspect and copy official records
of the association. A request must be honored within 10 business days. Certain records
(like attorney-client communications) may be exempt.

## How much notice is required for board meetings?
At least 48 hours posted notice is required for board meetings. Annual meetings require
14 days' written notice mailed or delivered to each owner.

## What is an estoppel certificate?
An estoppel certificate states what a unit owner owes the association at a specific point in
time. It is commonly required when a unit is sold. Under Florida law, the association must
provide one within 10 business days of a request; the fee is capped by statute.

## Are there limits on rental restrictions?
Under Florida law, amendments to the Declaration that restrict rentals only apply to owners
who acquired their unit after the amendment was recorded, unless they voted for it. Check
your Declaration for the current rental rules and any grandfathering provisions.

## What are the reserve requirements?
Florida condominiums are required to fund reserves for major components (roof, painting,
paving, etc.) unless owners vote to waive or reduce them. Since 2025, buildings 3 stories
or taller must conduct structural integrity reserve studies and fully fund those reserves.

## What are my rights if I disagree with the board?
Owners may petition for a special meeting (at least 20% of owners must sign). Disputes can
be submitted to the Florida Division of Condominiums, Timeshares, and Mobile Homes for
non-binding arbitration before filing a lawsuit.
