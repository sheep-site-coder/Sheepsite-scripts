# Florida Condominium Law — Resident FAQ
# Source: Florida Statute Chapter 718 (Condominium Act)
# Last updated: March 2026 — reflects amendments through 2024 legislative session (HB 1021)
# Note: Florida condo law has been amended every session since 2022. Verify critical details
# with a Florida-licensed community association attorney for specific legal situations.

---

## What law governs Florida condominiums?
Florida Statute Chapter 718 (the "Condominium Act") governs all condominiums in Florida.
The association must operate in compliance with both Chapter 718 and its own governing documents
(Declaration, Bylaws, Articles of Incorporation, and Rules). Where they conflict, Florida law prevails.

---

## BOARD MEETINGS

## How often must the board meet?
Associations with more than 10 units must hold board meetings at least **quarterly** (4 times per year).
[s. 718.112(2)(c)]

## How much notice is required for a board meeting?
**Regular board meetings:** Notice must be posted conspicuously on the condominium property at least
**48 continuous hours** before the meeting. The notice must list all agenda items.

**Meetings involving special assessments or rule changes affecting unit use:** These require a separate
notice of at least **14 days**, either mailed or posted conspicuously on the property, in addition to
the standard 48-hour posting.

## Where must board meetings be held?
**Annual and membership meetings** must be held within **45 miles of the condominium property**,
unless the bylaws specify a different location. Regular board meetings have no specific location
requirement by statute.

## Can board members participate by phone or video?
Yes. Board members may participate via **telephone, real-time videoconferencing, or similar
real-time electronic communication**. Remote participation counts toward quorum. However,
the physical meeting location must remain accessible to unit owners who wish to attend.

## Are board meetings open to residents?
Yes — board meetings are **open to all unit owners**. The only permitted closed-session topics are:
- Meetings with the association's attorney regarding **pending or proposed litigation**
- Discussion of **personnel matters**

For all other topics, owners have the right to attend.

## Can I speak at a board meeting?
Yes. Unit owners have a **statutory right to speak on all designated agenda items** before the board
votes on them. The board may impose reasonable rules (e.g., a 3-minute time limit per speaker) but
cannot eliminate the right to speak entirely.

## Can I record a board meeting?
Yes. Unit owners may **audio record or videotape** any board or membership meeting. This right
cannot be taken away. The Division may adopt rules about where recording equipment may be placed,
but cannot prohibit recording altogether.

## Can I get an item added to the board meeting agenda?
Yes. If **20% of all voting interests** sign a petition requesting that a specific item be addressed,
the board must place it on the agenda at its **next regular board meeting within 60 days** of
receiving the petition.

## ANNUAL MEETINGS AND ELECTIONS

## How much notice is required for the annual meeting?
At least **14 days** written notice, including the full agenda, must be mailed, hand-delivered,
or electronically transmitted to each unit owner. The notice must also be posted conspicuously
on the property at the same time.

## How are board elections conducted?
- Elections are conducted by **secret ballot**
- At least **20% of eligible voters must cast a ballot** for a valid election; if this threshold
  is not met, candidates run unopposed and are deemed elected without a vote
- **General proxies may not be used** for board elections; limited proxies are allowed for
  other membership votes
- Any eligible unit owner may run for the board; association employees are not eligible
- A person convicted of a felony is ineligible to serve unless civil rights were restored
  at least 5 years prior

## Is there a term limit for board members?
Yes. Since 2024, board members may not serve **more than 8 consecutive years** unless approved
by an affirmative vote of **two-thirds of all votes cast** at the election. [HB 1021, 2024]

## Are newly elected board members required to get training?
Yes. Within **90 days** of election, every new director must:
1. Submit a written certification that they have read and understood the governing documents
   and will uphold their fiduciary duties, AND
2. Complete a **4-hour educational curriculum** covering structural integrity, elections, and
   financial literacy.

Failure to meet this requirement within 90 days results in the director being **suspended from
the board** until the requirement is completed. [HB 1021, 2024]

## RESIDENT RIGHTS AND MEETING PARTICIPATION

## Can I petition for a special membership meeting?
Yes. If **10% of all voting interests** sign a petition requesting a special meeting, the board
must call and hold the meeting within **60 days** of receiving the petition.

## Can the board suspend my right to use the pool, gym, or other amenities for a violation?
Yes, but with important limits. The association may suspend use of **common elements and
facilities** for rule violations or non-payment. However, suspensions **may never apply to**:
- Your **assigned parking space** (if there are no other parking options available to you)
- **Elevators** (necessary for access)
- **Utility services**
- Any essential access to and from the property

## Can the board suspend my voting rights?
Yes, but only for delinquencies exceeding **$1,000** that remain unpaid for more than **90 days**.
The association must give **30 days' written notice** before the suspension takes effect.
While suspended, those voting interests are excluded from quorum calculations.

## FINES AND ENFORCEMENT

## What is the maximum fine the board can levy?
Fines are capped at **$100 per violation per day** (for continuing violations), with a maximum
aggregate of **$1,000 per violation**. A fine cannot exceed these limits unless otherwise
permitted by statute. [s. 718.303]

## What process must the board follow before fining me?
The board must:
1. Vote to levy the fine
2. Provide **at least 14 days' written notice** specifying the violation and proposed fine
3. Offer you a **hearing before a fining committee** — a committee of at least **3 unit owners**
   who are not officers, directors, or employees of the association
4. The committee may only confirm or reject the fine — it cannot change the amount
5. If the committee rejects the fine by majority vote, **the fine cannot be imposed**
6. If confirmed, payment is due within **5 days** of the committee's decision

If you did not receive proper written notice and a hearing opportunity, the fine is
procedurally defective and may be challenged.

## What counts as election fraud?
Florida law (as of 2024) explicitly makes ballot tampering, coercion, and bribery related to
condo elections **first-degree misdemeanors**. [HB 1021, 2024]

## OFFICIAL RECORDS AND DOCUMENT ACCESS

## What records must the association keep?
The association must maintain official records including:
- Declaration, Bylaws, and Articles of Incorporation (permanently)
- Rules and regulations
- Minutes of all board and membership meetings
- Current roster of all unit owners (with mailing addresses and email, if provided)
- Insurance policies
- All contracts for work to be performed
- Accounting records (receipts, expenditures, assets, liabilities)
- Bank statements and reconciliations
- Invoices and cancelled checks
- Audits, financial reviews, and monthly financial statements
- Election ballots, proxies, and sign-in sheets (retained for **1 year**)
- Milestone inspection reports and structural integrity reserve studies (**15 years**)
- All other written records related to the operation of the association

Most financial and operational records must be retained for a **minimum of 7 years**.

## How do I request association records?
Submit a **written request** to the association. You do not need to state a reason or justify
your request. The association must make records available within **10 working days** (not
calendar days). After the 10-working-day period, failure to produce creates a rebuttable
presumption of willful non-compliance.

## What can the association charge me for copies?
Associations may charge a **reasonable copying fee** (typically no more than **25 cents per page**
for paper copies). Importantly, if you bring your own device (phone, scanner) to inspect records,
the association **cannot charge you** for the time spent scanning — you can photograph records
for free.

## What records can the association refuse to show me?
Exempt records include:
- Attorney-client privileged communications and attorney work product
- Personnel records of association employees (other than total compensation)
- Medical records of owners or employees
- Social Security numbers, driver license numbers, credit card or bank account numbers
- Security system information (passwords, codes, access details)
- Information related to pending litigation that would otherwise be privileged

## What happens if the association refuses to provide records?
Starting on the **11th working day** after your request, you begin accruing **$50 per calendar day**
in statutory damages, for up to 10 days (maximum $500 in statutory damages), plus actual damages
and reasonable attorney's fees if you prevail. Directors or managers who repeatedly violate records
access requirements commit a **misdemeanor of the second degree**. Willfully destroying records to
conceal mismanagement is a **first-degree misdemeanor**. [s. 718.111(12)]

## WEBSITE REQUIREMENTS

## Does the association have to maintain a website?
- **Currently (through December 31, 2025):** Associations managing **150 or more units** must
  maintain a website.
- **Effective January 1, 2026:** The threshold drops to **25 or more units** — meaning virtually
  all Florida condo associations will be required to maintain a compliant website.
[s. 718.111(12)]

## What must be on the association's website?
All required documents are posted on a **password-protected section** accessible only to unit
owners and employees. Required documents include:
- Declaration, Bylaws, Articles of Incorporation, and all amendments
- Rules and regulations
- Current year's budget and proposed budgets
- Monthly financial statements
- All contracts and bids (for contracts exceeding 5% of annual budget)
- Board and membership meeting notices and agendas
- Minutes of all board and membership meetings (7 years)
- Financial reports and audits (7 years)
- Names and contact information for all board members
- Director certifications and conflict of interest disclosures
- Structural integrity reserve studies
- Milestone inspection reports
- All current insurance policies
- Building permits

**Posting deadlines:** Membership meeting notices must be posted at least **14 days** before the
meeting; board meeting notices at least **48 hours** before the meeting. Documents to be
considered at a meeting must be posted at least **7 days** before the meeting.

**What must be redacted before posting:** Social Security numbers, driver license numbers, credit
card and bank account information, emergency contact information, attorney-client materials, and
security system details must be removed from all posted documents.

## STRUCTURAL INTEGRITY AND BUILDING SAFETY

## What is a Structural Integrity Reserve Study (SIRS)?
A SIRS is a professionally conducted study — by a licensed architect or engineer — assessing the
condition and remaining useful life of a building's major structural components, and calculating
the reserve funding needed to repair or replace them. It was created by Florida law in 2022 in
direct response to the Champlain Towers South collapse in Surfside (June 2021, 98 deaths).
[s. 718.112(2)(g); SB 4-D, 2022]

## Which buildings require a SIRS?
All residential condominium buildings **three stories or higher** must have a SIRS completed.

## What are the SIRS deadlines?
- Buildings that existed before July 1, 2022: SIRS must have been completed by **December 31, 2024**
  (or by December 31, 2026 if the building also required a milestone inspection)
- After the initial study: must be updated at least **every 10 years**

## What does the SIRS cover?
At minimum: roof, load-bearing walls and primary structural members, floors, foundation,
fireproofing and fire protection systems, plumbing, electrical systems, waterproofing and
exterior painting, windows and exterior doors, and **any other item** with a deferred
maintenance or replacement cost exceeding **$10,000**.

## Can owners vote to waive SIRS reserve funding?
**No.** This is a hard prohibition. Owners may **not** vote to waive or reduce reserve funding
for any components identified in the SIRS. This overrides the prior common practice of owners
voting each year to waive reserves (which kept assessments low but deferred critical maintenance).
This waiver ban does not apply to reserves for items not covered by the SIRS.

## What is a Milestone Inspection?
A separate requirement for buildings **3 or more stories** that have reached age **30** (or age
**25** if within 3 miles of the coastline). Phase 1 is a visual inspection by a licensed
architect or engineer. If no substantial structural deterioration is found, Phase 2 is not
required. If deterioration is found, Phase 2 involves more detailed (potentially destructive)
testing. Local building departments enforce compliance; non-compliant buildings may be deemed
unsafe for occupancy. [s. 553.899]

## BUDGET AND ASSESSMENTS

## How much notice must be given before the budget meeting?
The board must deliver or mail each unit owner a **notice of the budget meeting and a copy of
the proposed annual budget** at least **14 days** before the meeting. The notice must also be
posted conspicuously on the property.

## Can I challenge a large assessment increase?
Yes — the "115% rule." If the board adopts a budget requiring assessments that exceed **115% of
the prior year's assessments**, unit owners may challenge it:
1. Gather signatures from at least **10% of all voting interests** within **21 days** of the
   budget meeting
2. The association must hold a **special membership meeting**
3. At that meeting, owners may adopt a substitute budget by a **majority vote of all voting
   interests** (not just those present)

**Important exception:** The 115% override right does NOT apply to increases driven by mandatory
SIRS reserve funding — those reserves must be fully funded regardless of the percentage increase.

## ESTOPPEL CERTIFICATES

## What is an estoppel certificate?
An estoppel certificate states what a unit owner owes the association at a specific point in time —
including assessments, fines, and any outstanding violations. It is required in most real estate
sales and mortgage transactions. [s. 718.116(8)]

## How quickly must the association provide one?
Within **10 business days** of a written request.

## How long is an estoppel certificate valid?
- Delivered by **hand or email:** valid for **30 days**
- Delivered by **regular mail:** valid for **35 days**

## What fees can the association charge for an estoppel certificate?
| Situation | Maximum Fee |
|---|---|
| No delinquency | $250 |
| Expedited (within 3 business days) | +$100 (up to $350) |
| Delinquency exists | +$150 (up to $400) |
| Multiple units, same request | Capped: $750 (up to 25 units), $1,000 (26-50), $1,500 (51-100), $2,500 (101+) |

If a buyer or lender relies in good faith on an estoppel certificate, the association **waives
the right to collect** any amounts exceeding what was stated in the certificate, for the full
validity period.

## ELECTRONIC NOTICES AND VOTING

## Can the association send me notices by email?
Only if you have **consented in writing** (or electronically) to receive notices by email.
Consent is revocable at any time. Without consent, the association must mail or hand-deliver
required notices.

## Can I vote online in association elections?
Yes, if the association has adopted an online voting system and you have individually consented.
The system must authenticate your identity, prevent double-voting, issue a receipt confirming
your vote, and permanently separate your identity from your ballot to preserve secrecy for
board elections. The board must give at least **14 days' notice** before adopting an online
voting resolution. [s. 718.128]

## DISPUTE RESOLUTION

## What can I do if I disagree with the board?
Options include:
- **Petition for a special meeting** (10% of owners)
- **File a complaint** with the Florida Division of Condominiums, Timeshares, and Mobile Homes
- **Non-binding arbitration** through the Division (required before filing a lawsuit in most cases)
- **Presuit mediation** (required for certain SIRS-related disputes)
- **Civil lawsuit** (after exhausting required pre-suit procedures)

## Am I protected if I file a complaint against the association?
Yes. Florida's 2024 amendments added **anti-SLAPP protections** for unit owners who file
legitimate complaints against their associations. The association cannot retaliate against you
for exercising your legal rights. [HB 1021, 2024]
